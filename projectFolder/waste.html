Recycling and Compost


Every year, the world generates over 2 billion tons of municipal solid waste. This waste can heap up in landfills, or even worse, contribute to pollution in the ocean and other natural habitats. So, how can we minimize our waste?


Recycling

Some tips on recycling:

  • Correct recycling practices: keep recyclables clean and dry; do not mix different recyclable materials; know which plastics you can recycle (rigid plastics with resin codes 1-7) and which you cannot; no bags!
  • Buy recyclABLE materials
  • Buy recyclED materials
  • Do not wishcycle (add non-recyclable materials into recycling bins), it ruins the whole batch of recycling

Compost

How to successfully compost:

  • Find an ideal spot for your compost with even land, some (but not too much) sunlight, and a drainage system
  • Maintain the correct amount of moisture - it should be about as damp as a wrung-out sponge
  • Have a good ratio of green materials (nitrogen) to brown materials (carbon) - it is generally advised to have about 1/3 greens and 2/3 browns
  • Chop up larger pieces before adding them to the compost
  • Regularly turn and mix compost to aerate it and help it decompose
  • Add layers of greens and browns
